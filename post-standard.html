Coming out of an off-camber corner on the roads high above Los Angeles, the back tire of the $26,000 Kawasaki H2 breaks loose and starts sliding. The superbike shimmies across the pavement, skirting the ragged edge of the road as rocks and weeds whack against my Alpinestars leathers.

I regain control of the rear end, slowly let out a sigh of relief, and ease from the side of the bike back onto its seat. The supercharger whines as I slowly twist the throttle, but I hold steady halfway through the rev range, completely frozen, unsure of what to do.
